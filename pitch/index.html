License FAQ

straight answers.

Can I use the free version for a paying client?
Yes — MIT license. Build for clients, charge for your time. Just keep the Plated branding visible on the site.
Does white-label mean I own the code?
You get a perpetual commercial license. You don’t own the IP, but you can ship it under your brand forever — no expiry, no renewal required.
What counts as a “major version”?
Semantic versioning — v1→v2, v2→v3. Patch and minor releases (v1.1, v1.2.3) are always free on GitHub.
Is the license per client or per developer?
Per developer or agency. One $299 license covers unlimited client sites — no per-seat or per-site fees.
What if I don’t buy the v2 update?
Nothing bad happens. Your v1 white-label license is valid forever. You stay on v1 and pull free patches from GitHub as usual.
Is the CulinaryOS MCP integration in the free version?
Yes. The MCP integration is part of the open-source core — free for everyone, forever.
